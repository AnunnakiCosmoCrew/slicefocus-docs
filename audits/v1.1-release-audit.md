## 1. Release Overview

SliceFocus v1.1 is the first full release cycle tracked end-to-end on GitHub Projects (migrated from Linear on Mar 17). The release covers both the **Java/Spring Boot backend** (BE) and the **Flutter frontend** (FE), culminating in macOS and iOS App Store submissions.

### Highlights

- **134 issues completed** across both repos — all in Done status, zero remaining.
- **354 story points** delivered in 28 calendar days.
- **98 PRs merged** (48 BE + 50 FE) with a **100% CI success rate**.
- **Major features shipped:** APNS Live Activity push notifications, Google Cloud Tasks integration, account deletion, WebSocket real-time events, auto-advancing Pomodoro phases, macOS menu bar support, App Store releases (macOS + iOS).
- **Release tags:** `be/v4.0.4`, `fe/v1.0.0`, `fe/v1.1.0`

## 8. DORA Metrics — Industry Benchmark

[DORA](https://dora.dev) (DevOps Research and Assessment, now part of Google Cloud) identified four key metrics that predict software delivery performance. Published annually in the *State of DevOps Report* since 2014, the research shows these metrics correlate with both business outcomes and team wellbeing.

### DORA Tier Definitions

| Metric | Elite | High | Medium | Low |
| -- | -- | -- | -- | -- |
| Deployment Frequency | On-demand (multiple/day) | Weekly–monthly | Monthly–biannually | < once per 6 months |
| Lead Time for Changes | < 1 hour | 1 day – 1 week | 1 week – 1 month | > 6 months |
| Change Failure Rate | 0–15% | 16–30% | 16–30% | > 30% |
| Mean Time to Restore | < 1 hour | < 1 day | < 1 week | > 6 months |

### SliceFocus v1.1 Assessment

| Metric | Measured Value | Tier |
| -- | -- | -- |
| Deployment Frequency | 6 prod deploys in 28 days (~weekly) | **High** |
| Lead Time for Changes | 18 min median PR merge → same-day deploy | **Elite** |
| Change Failure Rate | 0% (100% CI pass rate, no rollbacks) | **Elite** |
| Mean Time to Restore | No incidents recorded | N/A |

Three out of three measurable metrics place at Elite or High tier. The deployment frequency (weekly) falls just short of Elite (multiple per day / on-demand), but the gated promotion pipeline (dev → test → prod) is a deliberate quality gate rather than a bottleneck.

## 10. Executive Summary & Recommendations

### What Went Well

- **Complete delivery:** 134/134 issues shipped, 354 story points, zero items left behind.
- **CI maturity leap:** From no PR checks to 2 required CI gates + CodeQL + monthly OWASP scans — **100% pass rate** across 100+ runs.
- **Faster cycle times:** Mean lead time dropped 43% (1.57 days → 0.9 days) vs the v1.0 audit period.
- **Smaller PRs:** Average PR size cut from ~230 to ~98 additions — more reviewable, less risky.
- **Automated review adoption:** Copilot reviews every PR with active developer engagement (up to 10 replies per PR). A major process improvement from zero reviews in v1.0.
- **Release management established:** Release tags, image-based promotion pipeline, gated deployments (12 dev → 6 test → 6 prod).
- **Estimation discipline:** 99.3% of issues have story point estimates (up from 90%).

### What Needs Attention

- **FE test step is non-blocking** (`continue-on-error: true`) — test failures silently pass CI.
- **Post-deploy verification absent** — no smoke test or health check after any deployment.
- **FE branch accumulation** — 105 local / 199 remote branches need cleanup.
- **`FocusSession.status` still a String** — partially addressed from v1.0 audit.
- **Priority field underused** — only 22% of issues have a priority set.
