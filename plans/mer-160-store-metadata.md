### Description (4000 chars max)

```
SliceFocus shows your entire day as a circular clock face — one glance tells you where your time goes, and a built-in Pomodoro timer keeps you focused while you follow your plan.

PLAN YOUR DAY VISUALLY
Your 24-hour schedule appears as an interactive radial chart. Each task is a colored arc on the circle. Unplanned time shows as empty gaps, making wasted hours immediately obvious.

Create tasks by drawing directly on the clock face — press and sweep to define a time block, then name it. No menus, no complex forms. Adjust your plan throughout the day by editing or resizing any block.

FOCUS WITH POMODORO
Tap any task to start a focus session. The classic Pomodoro cycle is built in: 25 minutes of focused work, followed by a 5-minute break. After every 4 cycles, take a longer 15-minute break. Pause, resume, or stop at any time.

When your scheduled time for a task ends, the session stops automatically — SliceFocus respects your plan boundaries.

STAY ON TRACK
- Push notifications alert you when a focus phase ends
- Menu bar timer on macOS shows your countdown while you work in other apps
- Lock screen timer on iOS keeps your progress visible without unlocking
- Real-time sync keeps all your devices up to date

TRACK YOUR PRODUCTIVITY
Review your session history to see how you spent your time. The analytics dashboard shows completed sessions, total focus minutes, and daily trends. The actual-vs-planned report compares what you planned against what you actually did — so you can improve over time.

FEATURES
- 24-hour radial schedule with drag-to-create
- Built-in Pomodoro timer (25/5 work-break cycles)
- Push notifications for phase transitions
- macOS menu bar timer
- iOS lock screen timer
- Session history and analytics
- Actual vs planned productivity report
- Weekly and monthly trend tracking
- Light and dark themes
- Soundscape catalog for ambient focus audio
- Multi-device sync
- Google and Apple Sign-In

SliceFocus is for anyone who wants to see their time clearly and protect their focus — students, remote workers, freelancers, or anyone building better daily habits.
```
